Next Generation NCLEX Question Types

Learn about the new question formats introduced in the NGN and how to approach them.

What is the Next Generation NCLEX (NGN)?

Starting April 1, 2023, the NCLEX exam introduced new question types designed to better assess clinical judgment skills. These new formats are collectively known as Next Generation NCLEX (NGN) questions.

NCSBN Clinical Judgment Measurement Model

The NGN questions are based on the NCSBN Clinical Judgment Measurement Model (NCJMM), which identifies six cognitive skills used in clinical decision-making:

1

Recognize Cues

Identifying relevant information from the client situation

2

Analyze Cues

Making sense of recognized information and comparing to standards

3

Prioritize Hypotheses

Evaluating and ranking potential client problems

4

Generate Solutions

Identifying potential actions to address the client problems

5

Take Action

Implementing interventions in a prioritized sequence

6

Evaluate Outcomes

Comparing client response to expected outcomes

New NGN Question Types

Extended Multiple Response

These questions require you to select all applicable options from a list that may contain more options than traditional NCLEX questions. They use a new scoring method that awards points for correct selections and may deduct points for incorrect selections.

Example:

A nurse is assessing a client diagnosed with sepsis. Which clinical manifestations would the nurse expect to find? Select all that apply.

Tips for Success:

  • Read each option carefully and independently
  • Don't assume a specific number of correct answers
  • Consider each option as either correct or incorrect based on the scenario
  • Be aware of the +/- scoring method, which awards points for correct selections and deducts points for incorrect selections

Drag-and-Drop/Bowtie

These items require moving options (tokens) to appropriate placeholders. The bowtie format specifically asks you to identify a client condition, related nursing actions, and evaluation parameters.

Example:

A client is admitted with severe dehydration following three days of vomiting and diarrhea. The client has dry mucous membranes, poor skin turgor, and a heart rate of 124 beats per minute.

Identify the client's most likely condition, two nursing actions to address this condition, and two parameters to monitor the client's progress.

Note: On the actual NCLEX, you would drag options from a list to placeholders in a bowtie-shaped diagram.

Tips for Success:

  • First identify the central client problem based on the scenario
  • Consider which interventions specifically address that problem
  • Think about what parameters would indicate improvement or deterioration
  • Remember that options can typically only be used once
  • Look for clear connections between the condition, actions, and evaluation parameters

Cloze (Drop-down)

These questions require you to complete sentences by selecting the correct options from drop-down menus within the text. This format tests your understanding of relationships between concepts.

Example:

A client is admitted with suspected heart failure. The nurse would expect to find which assessment findings?

Complete the sentence by selecting the correct options:

The client with heart failure would likely present with and due to , requiring the nurse to monitor .

Tips for Success:

  • Read the entire sentence or paragraph first to understand the context
  • Consider the relationships between the different drop-down selections
  • Remember that all parts must be correct to receive full credit
  • Pay attention to qualifiers and time-sequence words that might affect your choices

Highlight

These questions require you to identify relevant information by highlighting sections of a medical record or scenario. This tests your ability to recognize important cues in complex clinical documentation.

Example:

A client presents to the emergency department with chest pain. Review the following section of the client's medical record and highlight the findings that suggest acute coronary syndrome.

Medical History: Hypertension for 10 years, Type 2 diabetes diagnosed 5 years ago, Hyperlipidemia

Current Medications: Metformin 1000 mg BID, Lisinopril 20 mg daily, Atorvastatin 40 mg daily

Presenting Symptoms: Client reports crushing substernal chest pain radiating to left arm and jaw that began 2 hours ago while walking. Pain is rated 8/10. Client also reports shortness of breath, nausea, and diaphoresis.

Vital Signs: BP 162/94, HR 102, RR 24, Temp 98.6°F, O2 sat 94% on room air

Labs: Troponin I: 0.09 ng/mL (normal range: <0.04 ng/mL), CK-MB: 8.2 ng/mL (normal range: <6.0 ng/mL)

Tips for Success:

  • Carefully read the question to understand what you're being asked to identify
  • Review the entire document before making your selections
  • Look for abnormal values, relevant symptoms, or risk factors related to the question
  • Be selective - highlighting too much information may count against you

Matrix/Grid

These questions require you to complete tables by selecting appropriate cells. This format tests your ability to identify relationships between multiple concepts or variables.

Example:

A nurse is caring for clients with various cardiovascular conditions. Match each condition with its characteristic assessment finding and priority intervention.

Cardiovascular Condition Characteristic Assessment Finding Priority Nursing Intervention
Hypertensive Crisis Blood pressure 220/118 mmHg with headache Administer antihypertensive medication as ordered
Acute Myocardial Infarction Crushing substernal chest pain with radiation to left arm Administer oxygen and aspirin as ordered
Heart Failure Bilateral crackles, JVD, and peripheral edema Position client in high Fowler's position

Tips for Success:

  • Read the question carefully to understand if you need to select one option per row/column or multiple options
  • Analyze each row and column intersection independently
  • Consider the relationships between the concepts in the rows and columns
  • Look for patterns in the information to help organize your thinking

Trend Item

These items present client data at different time points, requiring you to analyze changes and make clinical judgments based on trends. This tests your ability to recognize deterioration or improvement in a client's condition.

Example:

A nurse is caring for a postoperative client who had abdominal surgery 12 hours ago. Review the client's vital signs and assessment data over time and identify concerning trends.

Assessment 8:00 AM 12:00 PM 4:00 PM
Temperature 98.6°F (37°C) 99.2°F (37.3°C) 101.2°F (38.4°C)
Heart Rate 86 beats/min 98 beats/min 112 beats/min
Blood Pressure 124/76 mmHg 118/74 mmHg 110/68 mmHg
Respiratory Rate 18 breaths/min 20 breaths/min 24 breaths/min
Pain Level 4/10 5/10 7/10
Wound Dressing dry and intact Small amount of serosanguineous drainage noted Moderate amount of purulent drainage, redness around incision

Tips for Success:

  • Compare data across all time points to identify patterns or trends
  • Look for values that are moving toward or away from normal ranges
  • Consider the clinical significance of the changes, not just the numerical differences
  • Focus on how the trends relate to the client's overall condition or diagnosis

NGN Scoring Methods

+/- Scoring

Used when: Candidates may select unlimited options

How it works: Points are awarded for each correct selection and deducted for each incorrect selection. The minimum score is 0.

Example: If a question has 3 correct answers out of 7 options, and you select 2 correct and 1 incorrect:

+2 (correct) - 1 (incorrect) = 1 point out of 3 possible points

0/1 Scoring

Used when: Candidates are instructed to select a specific number of options

How it works: Points are awarded for each correct selection, but no points are deducted for incorrect selections.

Example: If a question asks you to select the 3 best interventions, and you select 2 correct and 1 incorrect:

+2 points out of 3 possible points

Rationale Scoring

Used when: Items link a concept with its justification (like cloze items)

How it works: Both parts must be correct to receive full credit.

Example: In a sentence completion question where you must select both a condition and its cause, you only receive credit if both selections are correct.
