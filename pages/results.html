Question Analysis

Question 1 Correct
Pharmacological & Parenteral Therapies

A client with hypertension is prescribed lisinopril. Which of the following findings would warrant immediate notification of the healthcare provider?

Blood pressure of 136/84 mmHg
Decreased urinary output with dark amber urine
Heart rate of 88 beats/minute
Occasional dry cough

Explanation:

Decreased urinary output with dark amber urine could indicate acute kidney injury, which can be a serious side effect of lisinopril. This warrants immediate notification of the healthcare provider.

Question 2 Incorrect
Reduction of Risk Potential

The nurse is caring for a client who is 24 hours post-operative after a total hip replacement. The nurse should prioritize which of the following interventions?

Administering pain medication
Performing passive range of motion exercises
Encouraging deep breathing and coughing
Assessing for signs of deep vein thrombosis

Explanation:

Clients who have undergone orthopedic surgery, especially of the lower extremities, are at high risk for developing deep vein thrombosis. Assessment for DVT is a priority due to the potentially life-threatening complication of pulmonary embolism.
